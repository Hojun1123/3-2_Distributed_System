"I remember him being nothing but gracious." ', 'Dangerous Minds saw Pfeiffer play an ex-Marine who becomes a teacher working on a pilot programme for teenagers who are bright but underachieving, in a tough, inner-city school.', "Coolio's haunting track from it, which samples the Stevie Wonder song Pastime Paradise for its chorus, went on to become one of the most successful rap songs of all time, helping to bring the genre into the mainstream.", 'It continues to be widely listened to and has just passed a billion streams on Spotify, according to his official website.', 'The first verse begins with Coolio reciting a line from the bible - "<i class="ssrcss-xbdn93-ItalicText e5tfeyi2">As I walk through the valley of the shadow of death" - </i>before spinning off into a soul-baring confession which finds him questioning his actions and those around him, wondering if he will <i class="ssrcss-xbdn93-ItalicText e5tfeyi2">"live to see 24".</i>', 'The film <a href="https://www.boxofficemojo.com/release/rl709658113/weekend/" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">grossed nearly £85m</a> (£78m) worldwide and the track became the biggest-selling record of the year in the US, and Coolio was awarded the Grammy for best rap solo performance.', 'Snoop Dogg referenced the track in his tribute to the late star online, while sharing a picture of himself and Coolio posing on the set of the music video for Gangsta Walk - a track they collaborated on in 2006.', 'Fellow rapper and actor <a href="https://twitter.com/icecube/status/1575295135516020737" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">Ice Cube said</a>: "This is sad news. ', "Coolio started making music in the 80s, but he cemented his place in hip-hop history when he recorded Gangsta's Paradise.", 'A leading figure in the US West Coast rap music scene in the 90s, he was born in Pennsylvania, but grew up in the LA suburb of Compton, where his career flourished.', 'His rap moniker came from a conversation with a friend who asked him: "Who do you think you are, Coolio Iglesias?", <a href="https://www.blacknamesproject.com/people/coolio-artis-leon-ivey-jr" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">according to The Black Names Project website</a>.', 'He worked as a volunteer firefighter in the San Jose area before dedicated himself full-time to hip-hop.', 'In a 1994 interview with <a href="https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1994-07-24-ca-19374-story.html" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">Hthe Los Angeles Times</a>, he said he did so as "a way to clean up". "In firefighting training was discipline I needed," he said. "We ran every day.
