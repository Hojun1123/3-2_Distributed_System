['<b class="ssrcss-hmf8ql-BoldText e5tfeyi3">Workers at an Apple store in Maryland have voted to unionise, forming the tech giant\'s first retail union in the United States.</b>', 'The employees of the shop in Towson passed the measure 65-33, with about a dozen abstentions.', 'After the result came in, the group tweeted: "Now we celebrate… tomorrow we keep organising."', 'It is the third Apple store to launch a union drive this year, but the first to successfully hold a vote.', 'The new Apple Core union - short for the Coalition of Organized Retail Employees - penned an open letter to Apple in May, saying its bid was "about us as workers gaining access to rights that we do not currently have", but that it did not want to "go against or create conflict with our management".', 'Other Apple stores in Atlanta and New York, have also made moves toward unionisation. Staff in Atlanta, however, have delayed their planned ballot, with the union involved - the Communications Workers of America - <a href="https://cwa-union.org/news/organizing-update-157" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">alleging anti-union activity by the company</a>.', 'Unions are less common in the US than in many European countries, but are still protected in law. Forming one involves either the company voluntarily recognising a union, or workers gathering signatures from at least 30% of employees so <a href="https://www.nlrb.gov/about-nlrb/rights-we-protect/the-law/employees/your-right-to-form-a-union" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">that the National Labour Relations Board (NLRB) can hold a formal election</a>. ', 'News outlets have alleged that Apple has hired a law firm known for its union expertise, and <a href="https://www.vice.com/en/article/m7v8qp/leaked-memo-reveals-apples-anti-union-talking-points-for-store-managers" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">collated "talking points" for its management teams to dissuade employees</a> from signing up to one. ', 'In April, <a href="https://www.vice.com/en/article/jgmymy/leaked-audio-apple-vp-gives-apple-store-workers-anti-union-speech" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">Motherboard released an audio recording</a> of retail vice president Deirdre O\'Brien telling employees that while she recognised the right to join a union, "it\'s equally your right not to join a union".', '"I\'m worried about what it would mean to put another organization in the middle of our relationship, an organization that does not have a deep understanding of Apple or our business," the released audio says.', 'The employees in Towson had the backing of a long-established union, the International Association of Machinists and Aerospace Workers. Its president, Robert Martinez Jr, congratulated the Apple employees on what he called a "historic victory".', '"They made a huge sacrifice for thousands of Apple employees across the nation who had all eyes on this election," he said.', '"This victory shows the growing demand for unions at Apple stores and different industries across our nation."', 'Apple told the AFP news agency it was declining to comment on the vote, which still has to be officially certified by the NLRB.', "The Towson store's union is the latest in a string of high-profile union campaigns in the US.", 'In December, a successful campaign in New York saw <a href="https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-59588905" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">Starbucks employees form their first union at the coffee chain in decades</a>, which has sparked similar campaigns across many of the company\'s individual stores. ', 'And in April, <a href="https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-60944677" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">Amazon saw 55% of workers at a New York warehouse vote in favour of unionisation</a> - though Amazon is disputing that ballot and appealing for a re-run.
