['<b class="ssrcss-hmf8ql-BoldText e5tfeyi3">Plans for a takeaway in a South Yorkshire village have been refused to protect the public\'s waistlines. </b>', 'Permission was sought to turn part of a former school site on Nanny Marr Road, Darfield, into a takeaway. ', 'However, Barnsley Metropolitan Borough Council (BMBC) has ruled it would not be conducive to its "health and wellbeing agenda".', 'According to a council report, 9% of reception age children in Darfield are either obese or severely obese.', 'In addition, the council noted adult obesity rates in Barnsley are "exceptionally high" and there was already a high concentration of takeaways in the village.', 'The <a href="https://www.bbc.com/lnp/ldrs" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">Local Democracy Reporting Service</a>, which examined the report, said 10 letters of objection were received by the authority.', 'In the letters, residents raised concerns around late night noise, increased traffic, litter and vermin.
