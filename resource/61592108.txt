['<b class="ssrcss-hmf8ql-BoldText e5tfeyi3">A "vulnerable" coral species found in UK waters may be able to find new habitats due to the climate change, research has found.</b>', 'The pink sea fan lives in shallow waters from the western Mediterranean to north-west Ireland and the south-west of England and Wales.', 'The University of Exeter study found the species is likely to spread northwards as global temperatures rise.', 'The results could be used to identify priority areas to protect the coral.', 'The species is <a href="https://www.iucnredlist.org/fr/species/8262/12903486" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">classified as "vulnerable" worldwide</a> and is listed as <a href="http://publications.naturalengland.org.uk/publication/4958719460769792" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">a species of principal importance in England and Wales</a>.', 'Dr Tom Jenkins, from the University of Exeter, said: "Our future predictions... revealed an increase in suitable habitat for pink sea fans to the north of its current range -
