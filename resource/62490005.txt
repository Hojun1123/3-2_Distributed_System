['<b class="ssrcss-hmf8ql-BoldText e5tfeyi3">An artist has made patients\' scars "glisten with gold". </b>', "Carol Holliday, 65, from Cambridge, said she was inspired by a Japanese process called Kintsugi for the artworks on display at the city's Addenbrooke's Hospital.", 'The ancient technique involves <a href="https://www.bbc.com/travel/article/20210107-kintsugi-japans-ancient-art-of-embracing-imperfection" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">mending broken ceramics with gold</a>. ', "Ms Holliday worked with photographer Ryan Davies to take the patients' pictures and then applied the scars in those images with gold leaf. ", 'The psychotherapist and former University of Cambridge lecturer said: "When people talk about their emotional distress, they quite naturally talk in terms of being broken, fragmented, cracked, damaged... shattered, so it lends itself to Kintsugi.', '"It demonstrates powerfully and quite simply that despite what has happened, your experiences can be lived with and they can be lived with well."', 'She said the 11 patients involved were of all ages, ethnicities and had different scars.', "The 65-year-old said she had 100 patients, from Addenbrooke's and elsewhere, volunteer to be involved in the project.", 'Fiona Carey, who has metastatic kidney cancer, was among those who took part.', 'She said her inverted-V scar that goes from hip to hip and up to her sternum, which has been opened up four times, was "glistening now with gold".', '"It looks really quite amazing," said Ms Carey, who was photographed playing wheelchair basketball, which she plays in a national league.', '"I\'ve always been quite cool about my scars; I\'m genuinely quite proud of them as a sign of what I\'ve survived over the years.
