', '"They\'ve been keeping themselves to themselves."', 'Asian hornets, first sighted in Britain in 2016 in Tetbury, Gloucestershire, pose no greater risk to human health than native wasps and hornets.', 'However, they are known to raid honeybee nests and eat them.', 'The first sighting in Rayleigh was reported on Sunday, 25 September, and the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) issued a warning three days later to beekeepers in the wider area.', "It was estimated the nest took two months for the Asian hornets to build and it was discovered in Mr Holborn's garden on Thursday, before being destroyed with an insecticide.", 'Measuring 50cm long, it was taken on Saturday to the Fera Science centre in York, a laboratory managed by Capita and Defra.', 'Scientists will try to work out where the insects came from and whether Asian hornet queens have already left the nest and flown elsewhere.', 'The APHA inspectors will continue to monitor the area and lay small plastic traps for the insects.', 'Mr Holborn, who had the nest in his garden for an extra night before it was completely destroyed, said: "As a precaution, we did shut the window."', '<i class="ssrcss-xbdn93-ItalicText e5tfeyi2">Find BBC News: East of England on </i><a href="https://www.facebook.com/bbcnewseast/" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">Facebook</a><i class="ssrcss-xbdn93-ItalicText e5tfeyi2">, </i><a href="https://www.instagram.com/bbceastofengland/" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">Instagram</a><i class="ssrcss-xbdn93-ItalicText e5tfeyi2"> and </i><a href="https://twitter.com/bbclookeast" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">Twitter</a><i class="ssrcss-xbdn93-ItalicText e5tfeyi2">.
