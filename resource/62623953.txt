', 'A letter was sent by seven religious leaders to the town council, saying the statue was "offensive to God".', 'Richard Pears, former mayor of the town, said there were no plans to remove it or change its name.', 'The 36ft (11m) tall statue is part of a project to regenerate St Austell, which used to have a thriving china clay industry.', 'The letter said leaders of the town were "actively, though likely unknowingly, choosing to reject God and instead to bring the town under the spiritual influence of an \'earth goddess\'".', 'It went on: "We would ask that you consider either making significant changes to the statue… or at the very least the name is changed so that it is an abstract piece of art with no spiritual element.
