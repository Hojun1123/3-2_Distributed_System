['<b class="ssrcss-hmf8ql-BoldText e5tfeyi3">A nine-year-old boy and his father have pledged to reduce what goes into their black wheelie bin so it does not need emptying for six months.</b>', 'Jake and George Dowsett, of Rushden, Northamptonshire, came up with the idea after discovering how much soft plastic cannot be put in their recycling bin. ', 'The made the realisation while doing <a href="https://www.greenpeace.org.uk/resources/big-plastic-count-final-report/" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">Greenpeace\'s Big Plastic Count</a>. ', 'Jake said since beginning on 19 October, they "try not to buy products with excessive packaging".', 'George Dowsett said: "I\'d like to say I\'m not obsessed with it, but it does make you more conscious about environmental issues, it makes you think about it a lot more."', 'North Northamptonshire Council asks residents to put non-recyclable rubbish into <a href="https://www.northnorthants.gov.uk/bin-collection-services/what-goes-which-bin" class="ssrcss-k17ofw-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">a general refuse black wheelie bin</a>, including soft plastic such as carrier bags.  ', 'Some rigid plastic, like plastic bottles, margarine tubs and food trays, can go in recycling wheelie bins. ', 'Counting their plastic use for a week during the Big Plastic Count revealed "lots of our plastic was soft plastic", Jake said. ', 'His dad said: "We were just amazed at how many different types of plastic we were creating and we think we\'re fairly conscious about it anyway."', 'They now stuff a carrier bag with the soft plastic and take it to a supermarket collection point once a week. ', 'They have also changed their shopping habits by buying loose vegetables instead of pre-packaged ones.
